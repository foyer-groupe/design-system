Design Blog Foyer: What for?

Steven Lozach

Some years ago, the Design Team of Foyer didn't exist. It's only in 2016 that we start building a dedicated team to design and user research to make their needs at the center of our decision-making. In 4 years, we built a lot, we made mistakes, we had great successes. This blog is to share all of that with you.

Evolution of a Design Team

We started small, I was a contractor working from Paris and sometimes at Foyer Head Quarters. I quickly got support from some people within the company who share the same vision has mine.

Design is oftentime put on the side, in addition of some other "constraints" —like it's a constraint…— while it should be put right at the top of the first reflexions.

The team got the opportunity to grow up quickly, 3 new team members in 2017, and now we are 10. Yes, 10, I'm so proud of that, and glad that Foyer have trust in our vision!

Evolution of our Projects and Tools

As designers, we tend to find the right tools/solutions for the right needs. Some years ago developers would take all the design decisions by guessing user needs. No need to tell you that guessing is not the best way to get it done properly.

Today, we use and maintain a Design System to build user interfaces, design decisions are made after long phases of user research, or at least a way better understanding of our user needs. Almost all the projects start with a scrum framework and at least a designer in the team: this was not even imaginable some days ago.

The other big change is that we came from designing for one entity, Foyer Assurances, to 4 different Foyer entities: Wealins, CapitalatWork, Foyer Global Health, mostly for their internal tools and interfaces.

To be able to handle all of that, our tools evolved: we abandoned Sketch and Zeplin to give a chance to Figma, and we don't regret it. Figma allows a big scalability, the tool evolve in the good way, and the management of our design system is made much easier. And it's only an example among others.

Blog Post to Expect

Frankly? We don't know yet. Geoffrey proposed 2 blog posts to kick start the blog, and I'm sure our Designers would be glad to share with you all kind of content: Illustration research, Accessibility, user research, usability, etc.

Don't expect a steady pace from us, our team is quite busy, but we will put our best effort in it to bring new posts to you.
